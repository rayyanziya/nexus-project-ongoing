📋 Understanding the Nexus Data Map

This diagram shows all the information Nexus stores and how it connects together.

🧑‍💼 You are viewing this in  Business View — plain English, no tech jargon
🗂️

What are the boxes?

Each box is a type of data Nexus stores. For example: "Companies", "Projects", "Invoices". Think of them like folders in a filing cabinet.

↗️

What are the lines?

Lines show how data connects. A line from "Projects" to "Companies" means every project belongs to a company.

🎨

What do the colours mean?

Colours group related boxes by business area — e.g. everything blue is about Clients & Projects, everything yellow is about Billing.

🖱️

How do I explore it?

Click any box to see details. Scroll to zoom. Drag to move around. Use the left panel to filter by business area.

❓ How to read this diagram

Quick reference guide for both business and technical readers.

🧑‍💼

Business View

Shows each data type as a card with a plain-English description. Lines are labelled "has many", "belongs to", etc. Best for non-technical readers.

⚙️

Technical View

Shows every database column with its type and constraints (PK, FK, etc.). Best for developers and database administrators.

🔑

Technical badges

PK = Unique record ID  |  FK = Links to another table  |  UQ = Must be unique  |  ENC = Encrypted/private data

🏢

Business areas (colours)

Each colour represents a business function: Identity, AI Intake, Leads, Projects, Ticketing, Documents, Billing, Notifications, Monitoring.
